Building and Construction (Advanced) RPL

About The Qualification
  • Designed for small to medium builders: Meets the needs of builders and managers of small to medium-sized building businesses.
  • Managerial focus: Ideal for those overseeing building operations and site teams.
  • Licensed responsibility: Recognises the appropriately licensed person under the relevant state/territory building authority.
  • Broad applicability: Applicable across all Australian States and Territories.
Scope of Work

The Building and Construction scope of work includes but is not limited to:

  • Planning building or construction work
  • Supervising site communication and administration processes
  • Applying legal requirements to building and construction projects
  • Applying structural principles to residential and commercial constructions
  • Preparing simple building sketches and drawings
  • Minimising waste on the building and construction site
Experience Requirements

To be eligible, you must demonstrate:

  • At least 3 years’ relevant industry experience in building construction work
  • The majority of this experience obtained within the last 10 years
Eligibility

You’ll need to provide:

  • 100-point ID check (JP-certified)
  • Valid White Card (JP-certified)
  • Referee testimonial(s) confirming ≥ 3 years’ experience
  • Video or photo evidence of your work
Regulatory Licensing Requirements

According to NSW Fair Trading, tradies seeking a Building (general building work) License must meet one of two qualification criteria:

  • VET qualifications & units of competency:
    • CPC40120 Certificate IV in Building and Construction

    Plus any of the following:

    • CPC50220 Diploma of Building and Construction (Building)
    • CPC30220 Certificate III in Carpentry
    • CPC30111 Certificate III in Bricklaying/Blocklaying
    • Bachelor of Housing (Australian university) or a degree in civil engineering, structural engineering, architecture, housing, construction, construction management, construction project management, construction economics, applied science (building) or quantity surveying (Australian university)
  • University degree: A degree in building, construction, construction management, construction project management, construction economics, applied science (building), or quantity surveying from an Australian university requiring four years’ full-time study and a mandatory work placement (excludes associate or honorary degrees).

Builder licensing varies across States and Territories; additional requirements may apply.

Certificate IV in Building and Construction, commonly referred to as Cert 4 Building and Construction, is a nationally recognised qualification in Australia. It is designed for individuals who aspire to work in the building and construction industry or are already working in the field and want to enhance their skills and improve their credentials.

Important Information:
  • Nationally Recognised: Cert 4 in Building and Construction is recognised throughout Australia, making it a valuable qualification for those seeking a career in the construction sector.
  • Skill Enhancement: This course provides an opportunity to enhance your skills in key elements of building and construction, including project management, site supervision & compliance with relevant regulations.
  • Career Advancement: Completing Cert 4 Building and Construction can open doors for career advancement and may be a prerequisite for certain roles in the industry.

To enrol in Certificate IV in Building and Construction with your chosen RPL provider, there are specific entry requirements that applicants must meet. These requirements ensure that candidates have a basic foundation and understanding of the industry.

Important Information:
  • Minimum Age: Typically, candidates must be at least 18 years old to enrol in this course.
  • Language Proficiency: Proficiency in English is essential, as construction involves communication and documentation in English.
  • Work Experience: For RPL, 3+ years (relevant work experience in the construction industry). Some (non-RPL) may admit students with no prior experience.

Earning a Certificate IV in Building and Construction opens up various career opportunities in the construction industry. Graduates are equipped with the skills and knowledge needed for roles that involve planning, managing & overseeing construction projects.

Important Information:
  • Builder: Manage and coordinate construction projects.
  • Site Supervisor: Oversee daily site operations.
  • Construction Manager: Manage all aspects of a project from planning to execution.
  • Estimator: Assess project costs and prepare bids.
  • Building Inspector: Ensure compliance with codes and regulations.

The duration of completing Certificate IV in Building and Construction can vary depending on factors such as the mode of study, the institution you choose & the pace at which you learn.

Options:
  • Full-Time Study vs RPL: Full-time takes 6–12 months; RPL takes weeks to months.
  • Recognition of Prior Learning (RPL): If you have relevant work experience, RPL can significantly shorten the duration.
